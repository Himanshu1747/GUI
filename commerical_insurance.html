Commercial Liability

This is the base coverage for any buisness. Bodily injury and property damages for the following;

1. Premeise Liability ("slip & fall")

It makes no difference if this hazard was staged, or is real You will have to defend it regardless. Your Commercial Liability will provide your with a defense.

2. Products & Completed Operations

This covers your buisness in the event you, or your staff in advertantly contaminates food or beer, or maybe your supplier sold you contaminated material. You're business will be sued and win or lose you will have to defend it

3. Third Item

Personal and Advertising Injury. This portion covers your buisness in the event of Libel (false statements made in writing) Slander (false statements that are spoken) False arrest (mistakenly having someone arrested) Infringing on another’s copyright, Malicious prosecution, Use of another’s advertising idea, Wrongful eviction, entry or invasion of privacy.

4. Additional Coverages

Liquor Liability and Dram shop laws. The term “dram shop” refers back to when bars sold alcohol by the dram, a small serving typically less than a U.S. shot of liquor. A dram shop law is a state liquor law that places the blame for damages caused by intoxicated individuals on the establishment that served them. There are only six states that don’t have these laws on the books, they are; Delaware, Kansas, Maryland, Nevada, South Dakota, Virginia.
